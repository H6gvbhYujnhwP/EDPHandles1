# Cosma Website Analysis for EDP Handles

## Executive Summary

The Cosma website positions the Italian business as a **manufacturer-led, catalogue-oriented B2B brand**, whereas the current EDP Handles website functions as a **premium UK-facing landing page**. In practical terms, this means the EDP site currently conveys brand mood and trade intent well, but it does **not yet reflect the depth of the upstream Cosma offer** in terms of product taxonomy, finishes, systems, technical content, and downloadable resources.[1] [2]

The most important implication is strategic rather than cosmetic. If EDP Handles is a unique UK reseller and distributor, the next version of the site should evolve from a single-page luxury introduction into a **multi-page distributor website** that translates Cosma’s upstream structure into a clearer UK-market buying journey. That means separating **brand story**, **handle categories**, **open systems**, **express programme**, **finishes**, **catalogues**, and **trade contact paths** instead of keeping everything compressed into one homepage.[1] [2] [3] [4]

## What the Cosma Site Is Doing Structurally

Cosma’s primary navigation reveals a broad information architecture made up of corporate pages, product access points, finish references, downloadable resources, distributor information, and contact/support destinations. The visible core sections are **Profilo aziendale**, **Qualità e lavorazioni**, **Prodotti**, **Maniglie**, **Sistemi a giorno**, **Express**, **Finiture**, **Cataloghi**, **Distributori**, and **Contatti**.[1]

This structure matters because it shows that Cosma does not present itself only through inspiration or styling language. It presents itself through **catalogue logic**. The website supports product discovery by **type**, **category**, **finish**, and **programme availability**, which is a much more operational model than the current EDP Handles site.[1] [2] [5]

## Product Structure: What Cosma Reveals

On the main products page, Cosma separates the offer first by **Maniglie (225)** and **Sistemi (14)**, then further breaks the handle catalogue into families such as **Ponte**, **Ponte Modulari**, **Presa Chiusa**, **Presa Chiusa Modulare**, **Integrate**, **Integrate Modulari**, **Verticali**, **Verticali Integrate**, **Speciali**, **Elettrodomestici**, and **Pomoli**. The systems side includes **Sistemi** and **Sistemi Sottopensili**, while **Collezione Express (54)** is treated as a separate commercially meaningful grouping.[2]

This is an important insight for EDP. The current EDP Handles site uses mood-based collection labels such as “Modern Brass” and “Matte Steel,” which work well for premium presentation but do **not yet map cleanly** to Cosma’s underlying catalogue structure. If the aim is to represent the distributor offer accurately, the EDP site should eventually balance **inspirational positioning** with **usable product taxonomy**.[2]

## Range Names Identified That Could Be Showcased

The strongest named range list visible during review came from the **Sistemi a giorno** filtered view. The page exposes the following system or architectural storage ranges: **Tubi Appendiabiti**, **MIRA**, **TELA**, **ERRE 8**, **SYSTEM 16**, **FLAG**, **ELITE**, **INTERNA**, **USE IT**, **EOS**, **AURA**, **CUBO**, **FILO'**, and **FERE'**.[5]

These names are valuable because they are already legible product families rather than anonymous SKU codes. They offer a practical starting point for the UK site if EDP Handles wants to showcase **selected systems ranges** in a curated way.

By contrast, the visible handle area on the master products page exposes many product links primarily as **numeric identifiers**, which suggests the handle catalogue may need either catalogue/PDF support or a curated selection strategy before it can be translated cleanly into elegant UK-facing collection pages.[2]

## Finish Structure: A Major Gap on the Current EDP Site

Cosma’s finishes page is highly developed and organized by substrate family: **zamak**, **alluminio**, and **acciaio inox**. Within those families, the site lists many finish options across black, steel, bronze, brass, champagne, white, titanium, moka, tortora, chrome, and gunmetal-style tones.[4]

For EDP Handles, this is one of the clearest opportunities. A finishes page would help the site move from being merely aspirational to becoming genuinely useful for trade decision-making. Even if the full Cosma finish library is too broad for phase one, EDP should consider presenting a curated finish matrix such as **Brass / Bronze / Black / Steel / White / Special Order** and then expand later.[4]

## How the Current EDP Handles Site Compares

The current EDP site does several things well. It already communicates a premium Italian design story, trade orientation, and a curated atmosphere. It works as an introduction and as a brand positioning piece.

However, when compared with Cosma, the current site is missing four structural layers. First, it lacks a **page hierarchy**. Second, it lacks a **usable product taxonomy**. Third, it lacks a **finish reference structure**. Fourth, it lacks **technical/downloadable trade resources** such as catalogues, spec access, or programme-based navigation.[1] [2] [4]

## Recommended Range Strategy for EDP Handles

The safest route is **not** to publish the full Cosma catalogue immediately. Instead, EDP should launch a **curated distributor edit**.

A practical first-wave structure would be to present handles by **application family** rather than by raw Cosma internal filtering alone. For example, the site could group handle offerings into categories such as **Bridge Handles**, **Integrated Handles**, **Vertical Handles**, **Appliance Handles**, and **Knobs**, because those categories are understandable to UK designers and kitchen buyers while still aligning with Cosma’s catalogue logic.[2]

For systems, EDP can be much more explicit because the names are visible and usable. A first showcase shortlist could include **MIRA**, **TELA**, **ERRE 8**, **EOS**, **AURA**, and **CUBO**, with secondary ranges such as **SYSTEM 16**, **FLAG**, **ELITE**, **INTERNA**, **USE IT**, **FILO'**, **FERE'**, and **Tubi Appendiabiti** added if they are commercially relevant for the UK market.[5]

## Strategic Conclusion

The Cosma site confirms that EDP Handles has the basis for a much richer distributor website than the one currently built. The key decision is whether EDP wants to remain a **brand-introduction site** or become a **true UK catalogue-and-trade gateway**.

If the answer is the latter, the next phase should focus on turning the current luxury landing page into a **curated multi-page distributor site** with real structure: product families, selected ranges, finishes, downloadable resources, and stronger trade pathways. That would preserve the premium tone we already built while aligning much more closely with the upstream Cosma offer.[1] [2] [3] [4] [5]

## Recommended Next Discussion Points

The best next conversation is not about visual design first. It is about scope definition. In particular, we should decide which Cosma content EDP Handles will actually carry into the UK-facing experience.

| Decision area | Question to settle next |
|---|---|
| Product scope | Will EDP showcase only handles, or handles plus open systems? |
| Range depth | Will the site show a curated edit, or a broad catalogue? |
| Finish depth | Will finishes be shown as a simple palette or a detailed technical library? |
| Trade tools | Do you want downloadable catalogues/spec sheets on the site from phase one? |
| Commercial positioning | Should EDP be framed primarily as reseller, distributor, or exclusive UK partner? |
